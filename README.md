## Problem Statement:

After conducting research in repositories such as the UCI Machine Learning Repository and Kaggle, it was found that one of the main challenges in IoT for smart buildings is the underutilization of energy resources, due to the lack of connection between environmental conditions and the actual presence of people.

The main problem lies in the inefficient management of climate control and lighting in indoor spaces. Often, HVAC systems operate on fixed schedules rather than adjusting to actual demand, such as CO2 levels. This not only leads to unnecessary energy consumption but can also result in poor indoor air quality, which negatively impacts health and productivity.

## Justification

Building management faces a critical challenge due to the waste of resources in spaces that operate based on static logic. The importance of analyzing the data set from the Sutardja Dai Building lies in the transition toward the Smart Building model, where the Internet of Things (IoT) is no longer merely a data collection network but becomes a proactive decision-making system.

## Dataset

Each room includes 5 types of measurements: CO2 concentration, room air humidity, room temperature, luminosity, and PIR motion sensor data, collected over a period of one week from Friday, August 23, 2013 to Saturday, August 31, 2013.
